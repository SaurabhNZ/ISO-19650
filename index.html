1. What ISO 19650-1 is really about

ISO 19650-1 is the concept and principle part of the ISO 19650 series. In simple BIM language, it explains how project and asset information should be managed so the right people receive the right information, at the right level of detail, at the right time.

Purpose

Control information

Avoid uncontrolled model/file production. Information should respond to defined needs.

Method

Use agreed processes

Define responsibilities, delivery milestones, review states, approvals, and exchange rules.

Outcome

Support decisions

Information has value only when it supports design, construction, operation, maintenance, or asset decisions.

2. Core concepts — click each card

Information

Information is an asset

Models, drawings, schedules, documents, COBie/data, and records should be treated as managed project deliverables.

Practical BIM example: a door family is not enough. The door number, fire rating, hardware set, acoustic rating, and approved status may be the information needed for a specific decision.
Need

Start with requirements

Information production should begin from the appointing party’s needs, not from what the modeler feels like creating.

Example: the client needs FM handover data. That should influence naming, classification, parameters, model breakdown, and deliverable milestones from the start.
Quality

Check before sharing

Information should pass agreed checks before it is shared, published, or used for downstream decisions.

Example: clash-free does not mean information-ready. Naming, status, coordinates, classification, suitability, revision, and required attributes also need review.
Collaboration

Work in controlled states

Information moves through controlled containers and states such as work-in-progress, shared, published, and archived.

This reduces the risk that someone builds, prices, or coordinates against the wrong model or drawing issue.
Risk

Reduce ambiguity

The process reduces disputes caused by unclear responsibility, unreliable information, missing approvals, or inconsistent file naming.

The main BIM management value is not just 3D modelling; it is traceable, reliable information exchange.
Lifecycle

Think beyond design

Information management applies from strategic need through design, construction, handover, operation, and eventual renewal or disposal.

A model that is useful during design can become poor asset information if data requirements are ignored until the end.

4. Information requirements hierarchy

This is one of the most important ideas. Requirements flow down from the owner/client need into project and task-level deliverables.

OIR — Organizational Information Requirements

Information the organization needs to meet business objectives. Example: portfolio-level cost, compliance, carbon, maintenance, or space data.

AIR — Asset Information Requirements

Information needed to operate and maintain a specific asset. Example: maintainable equipment data, room data, warranty data, asset tags.

PIR — Project Information Requirements

Information needed to deliver a specific project. Example: design review deliverables, coordination models, cost plan data, consent documentation.

EIR — Exchange Information Requirements

What information must be exchanged, when, by whom, in what format, and for what purpose.

BIM manager test: If a model parameter does not support a requirement, decision, deliverable, or asset use case, question why it is being created.

5. Roles and responsibilities

Role termPlain BIM meaningTypical responsibility
Appointing partyUsually the client, owner, developer, or asset owner.Defines information needs, acceptance criteria, exchange requirements, and project outcomes.
Lead appointed partyMain consultant, contractor, or delivery lead appointed to manage information delivery.Plans, coordinates, and manages the information delivery process across task teams.
Appointed partyConsultant, subcontractor, designer, supplier, or specialist.Produces specific information deliverables according to the agreed scope and standards.
Task teamDiscipline or package team such as architecture, structure, MEP, façade, interiors, or civil.Creates, checks, and submits information containers for their assigned work.

6. Common Data Environment logic

The CDE is not just a folder system. It is a controlled workflow for information containers.

Work in Progress

Internal team development. Not suitable for wider reliance.

Shared

Checked enough for coordination or review by other parties, with clear suitability status.

Published

Authorized information for contractual, construction, procurement, or formal use.

Archived

Record of previous issues, decisions, revisions, and superseded information.

9. Glossary search

Information container
A named set of information such as a model, drawing, document, schedule, or dataset.
Level of information need
The right amount of geometry, data, and documentation required for a specific purpose.
CDE
Common Data Environment: the agreed environment and workflow for managing information.
Suitability status
A status that communicates what the information can be used for.
Revision
A controlled change record for an information container.
Information exchange
Formal transfer of defined information at a defined milestone.
Appointing party
The party requiring information and appointing others to produce it.
Lead appointed party
The party responsible for coordinating information delivery from appointed parties.

10. Practical takeaway for Revit / BIM teams

ISO 19650-1 is less about creating complex BIM documents and more about disciplined information management. For a Revit team, that means clear model purposes, clean naming, defined parameters, controlled exchanges, model status clarity, and reliable handover data.

Simple rule: model information should be intentional, traceable, checked, and useful.
